VGGT 3D Reconstruction - Consistency Test Comparison

Experiment Overview

Testing of VGGT's behavior when handling multi-view sketches where one view contains modifications (additions/deletions) not present in other views.

Observations

Addition Operations (9 cases)

  • Clean Integration (1/9): Plane wing cube - successfully added with proper 3D geometry
  • Fragmentation (3/9): Chair, Table, Armchair 1 - produced multiple independent single-view reconstructions rather than unified 3D models
  • View-Dependent Quality (4/9): Sofa, Armchair 2, Lwg, Plane Triangle - added elements appear 3D from certain angles, 2D-like from others
  • Geometric Distortion (1/9): Camera - addition caused twisted/distorted geometry

Deletion Operations (4 cases)

  • Deletion Ignored (2/4): Wagon wheels, House porch - targeted elements remained despite removal attempts
  • Fragmentation (1/4): Toothbrush - produced three separate single-view reconstructions
  • View-Dependent Deletion (1/4): Toothpaste cap - disappears from certain viewing angles, remains visible from others

Special Cases

  • Wagon Hole: Filling operation improved reconstruction quality (reduced fragmentation from 3 parts to 2 parts)
  • Quality Dependencies: Objects with poor original reconstructions (Savon, Table) showed degraded modification results

Key Findings

  1. Fragmentation is a major failure mode - affects 40% of cases (6/15)
  2. Fragmentation creates independent single-view reconstructions (typically matching input view count) rather than unified multi-view models
  3. View-dependent reconstruction quality - a common issue where modifications appear inconsistent across viewing angles
